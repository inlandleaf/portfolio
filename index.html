ZACHARY A. DANA

zdana@ucsc.edu

I am a Data Scientist & Engineering Intern at Vironix Health, where I focus on research and development of machine learning applications for chronic disease risk stratification. My recent projects involve integrating deep learning and survival analysis techniques for enhanced early intervention and novel risk factor identification in chronic kidney disease. In addition to my technical roles, I represented the company at SIAM's (Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics) annually hosted 2024 MPI (Mathematical Problems in Industry) conference, which addresses diverse problems in science and industry entailing solutions from basic physical modeling to advanced computation and data analysis.

Previously, I received an undergraduate degree in Network and Digital Technology from the Baskin School of Engineering at the University of California Santa Cruz, where I pursued coursework in both computer science and mathematics. My studies included software engineering, algorithms, machine learning, computer systems design, architecture, and networks, as well as linear algebra, calculus, discrete mathematics, and statistics.
